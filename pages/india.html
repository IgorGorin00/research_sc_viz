India

The logic behind the choice of SEZs is similar to the previous ones: the data is available from 1990 till 2019 (till 2021 for GRP), in order for the estimations to be accurate, a large pre-treatment period is needed. The source of data for this section is the “Handbook of statistics on Indian states” published by Reserve bank of India.

Following Special economic of zones were analyzed

Andhra Pradesh Special Economic Zone, Andhra Pradesh state

Andhra Pradesh SEZ was created in 2007. It is referred to as a multi-product SEZ. Residents of the SEZ include companies focused on IT / ITES (informational technology / informational technology enabled service), biotech, pharmaceuticals, building materials. (“SEZs in Andhra Pradesh”).

Mangalore Special Economic Zone, Karnataka state

Mangalore Special Economic Zone was created in 2007 and the area is more than 1600 acres. SEZ mainly hosts the petrochemical and chemical companies

Indore Special Economic Zone, Madhya Pradesh state

According to the SEZ website: “Indore SEZ was notified prior to the enactment of the SEZ Act’ 2005 and became operational W.E.F. 15.08.2003. This SEZ is the only Green Field multi product SEZ of India as on date and is spread over an area of more than 1100 hectares of land in Phase I & II . Industries of various sectors such as Plastic, Engineering, Pharmaceutical, Metal, Textile & Food processing etc. are located in the Zone.”

Falta Special Economic Zone, West Bengal state

Falta is a multi-product SEZ that was established in 2003. The enterprises in the zone include companies focusing on IT/ITES, Gems and Jewelry processing, Manufacture and Export of Aluminium.

Noida Special Economic Zone, Uttar Pradesh state

Noida SEZ was established in 2003 and it is a multi-product SEZ. The companies in the zone include companies focused on IT/ITES, Engineering And Related Industries, Handicraft, Gems And Jewellery, Pharmaceuticals.
